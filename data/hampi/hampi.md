# Hampi

## Description
Hampi is one of India’s most extraordinary historical and archaeological destinations located in the state of Karnataka. Once the grand capital of the Vijayanagara Empire, Hampi is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its massive temple complexes, ancient ruins, giant boulder landscapes, riverbanks, and rich South Indian history. The destination attracts backpackers, historians, photographers, spiritual travelers, and architecture enthusiasts from around the world. Hampi offers a surreal atmosphere where centuries-old ruins blend with dramatic rocky terrain and peaceful village life. The city is also known for its sunrise viewpoints, cycling routes, coracle rides, temple architecture, and relaxed backpacker culture, making it one of India’s most unique travel experiences.

## Best Time to Visit

- Recommended Months: October to February
- Why This Time is Best: The weather remains pleasant and suitable for exploring ruins, cycling, trekking, temple visits, and outdoor sightseeing. Summers in Hampi become extremely hot and dry, making daytime exploration difficult.

### Budget Breakdown (Realistic Expectations and observations)
A realistic traveler in Hampi can expect daily expenses around ₹6,500–₹7,500 ($75–$90), depending on accommodation style and sightseeing preferences. Budget backpackers can significantly reduce expenses by staying in hostels and renting bicycles or scooters for transportation. Monument entry fees are relatively affordable, although hiring local guides can slightly increase daily costs. Food expenses remain moderate because Hampi offers a combination of affordable South Indian eateries and tourist cafés serving international cuisine. Most sightseeing involves outdoor exploration across large archaeological zones, making transportation rentals useful for covering multiple locations efficiently. Travelers can reduce costs further by staying slightly outside the main tourist areas.

## Popular Food Items to Eat (max 5, least 3)
1. **South Indian Thali**  
   Traditional Karnataka-style meals served with rice, curries, sambar, chutneys, vegetables, and papad. These meals are affordable and widely available across Hampi.

2. **Masala Dosa**  
   Crispy rice crepes filled with spiced potato mixture and served with chutneys and sambar. It is one of the region’s most popular breakfast dishes.

3. **Banana Pancakes**  
   Hampi’s backpacker café culture has made banana pancakes one of the most famous café foods among international travelers.

4. **Filter Coffee**  
   Traditional South Indian filter coffee is widely enjoyed throughout Hampi’s cafés and local restaurants.

5. **Israeli and Continental Café Food**  
   Because of Hampi’s global backpacker popularity, many cafés serve shakshuka, hummus, pasta, smoothies, and international comfort foods.

## Things to Carry While Travelling and Advisories

### Travel Advisories (min 3, max 6)
- Hampi involves extensive walking, climbing, and outdoor exploration across rocky landscapes, therefore comfortable walking shoes are extremely important.

- Summers become extremely hot during daytime, so carrying water, sunscreen, and avoiding afternoon exploration is strongly recommended.

- Some ruins and viewpoints are located far apart, therefore renting bicycles or scooters can make sightseeing much more convenient.

- Mobile networks and ATM availability can occasionally become limited in certain backpacker-side areas, so carrying sufficient cash is advisable.

### Packing Checklist

#### Compulsory Items (min 3, max 5)
- Government ID / Passport
- Comfortable walking or trekking shoes
- Mobile phone and charger
- Water bottle
- Lightweight breathable clothing

#### Optional Items (max 8, least 4)
- Sunscreen
- Camera
- Power bank
- Sunglasses
- Hat or cap
- Small backpack
- Flashlight
- Basic medicines
