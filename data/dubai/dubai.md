# Dubai

## Description
Dubai is one of the world’s most futuristic and luxurious cities, located in the United Arab Emirates along the Persian Gulf coast. The city is globally famous for its towering skyscrapers, luxury shopping malls, desert adventures, artificial islands, and world-class entertainment. Dubai combines modern architecture, international business culture, Arabian heritage, and luxury tourism experiences, making it one of the most visited destinations in the Middle East. The city is home to iconic landmarks such as the Burj Khalifa, Palm Jumeirah, Dubai Marina, and Dubai Mall. Travelers visit Dubai for shopping, desert safaris, beach resorts, adventure activities, nightlife, fine dining, and large-scale entertainment attractions.

## Best Time to Visit

- Recommended Months: November to March
- Why This Time is Best: The weather remains pleasant and suitable for sightseeing, desert safaris, beach activities, outdoor dining, and city exploration during these months. Summers in Dubai become extremely hot with daytime temperatures often exceeding comfortable travel conditions.

### Budget Breakdown (Realistic Expectations and observations)
A realistic traveler in Dubai should expect daily expenses around AED 1,700–1,900 ($460–$520), depending on accommodation type and luxury preferences. Hotel stays form the largest portion of the budget because Dubai offers many premium and luxury properties near major tourist districts. Food expenses can vary widely, ranging from affordable food courts and local restaurants to extremely expensive fine-dining experiences. Public transportation is efficient and modern, although taxis and ride-sharing services can increase costs during peak travel periods. Activities such as desert safaris, theme parks, observation decks, and yacht cruises contribute significantly to daily spending. Travelers can reduce costs by staying outside central luxury districts and using Dubai Metro extensively.

## Popular Food Items to Eat (max 5, least 3)
1. **Shawarma**  
   One of Dubai’s most popular street foods consisting of grilled meat wrapped in flatbread with garlic sauce, vegetables, and pickles.

2. **Machboos**  
   A traditional Emirati rice dish prepared using spiced meat or seafood along with aromatic Middle Eastern spices and herbs.

3. **Luqaimat**  
   Sweet Emirati dumplings drizzled with date syrup or honey, commonly served during festivals and cultural events.

4. **Mixed Grilled Kebabs**  
   Dubai’s multicultural food culture makes grilled kebabs, hummus, and Middle Eastern platters highly popular among tourists.

5. **Camel Milk Desserts and Chocolates**  
   Specialty desserts and chocolates made using camel milk are unique food experiences associated with UAE culture.

## Things to Carry While Travelling and Advisories

### Travel Advisories (min 3, max 6)
- Dubai follows local UAE laws and cultural expectations, therefore respectful clothing and public behavior are recommended, especially near religious and traditional areas.

- Desert climates can become extremely hot during daytime, so hydration, sunscreen, and lightweight breathable clothing are essential.

- Public transportation is modern and efficient, therefore purchasing a Nol transport card can simplify travel across metro, buses, and trams.

- Many major attractions require advance online bookings during weekends, holidays, and tourist seasons because of heavy visitor demand.

### Packing Checklist

#### Compulsory Items (min 3, max 5)
- Passport and travel documents
- Comfortable walking shoes
- Mobile phone and charger
- Lightweight breathable clothing
- Sunscreen

#### Optional Items (max 8, least 4)
- Sunglasses
- Power bank
- Universal travel adapter
- Camera
- Light jacket for indoor air-conditioned areas
- Reusable water bottle
- Swimwear
- Small backpack
