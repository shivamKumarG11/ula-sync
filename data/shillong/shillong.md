# Shillong

## Description
Shillong is the capital city of Meghalaya in northeastern India and is widely known as the “Scotland of the East” because of its rolling hills, cool climate, waterfalls, pine forests, and scenic landscapes. The city is famous for its natural beauty, music culture, colonial-era charm, and peaceful atmosphere. Shillong serves as a major gateway for exploring Meghalaya’s mountains, caves, rivers, and living root bridges. The city is also known for its vibrant cafés, local Khasi culture, and frequent music festivals, making it highly popular among nature lovers, backpackers, photographers, and road-trip travelers. Shillong offers a refreshing combination of adventure, relaxation, and cultural exploration, especially for travelers seeking experiences beyond mainstream Indian tourist cities.

## Best Time to Visit

- Recommended Months: September to May
- Why This Time is Best: The weather remains cool, pleasant, and suitable for sightseeing, waterfalls, trekking, café visits, and road trips during these months. Monsoon season brings heavy rainfall that can affect outdoor activities and transportation.

A realistic traveler in Shillong can expect daily expenses around ₹7,000–₹8,000 ($85–$95), depending on accommodation style and nearby excursions. Food expenses are manageable because Shillong has both affordable local eateries and modern cafés serving international cuisine. Adventure activities and nearby day trips to Cherrapunji or Dawki can slightly increase overall daily spending. Travelers can reduce expenses by using shared cabs and booking accommodations in advance during tourist seasons.

## Popular Food Items to Eat (max 5, least 3)
1. **Jadoh**  
   A traditional Khasi rice dish prepared using rice, meat, herbs, and local spices. It is considered one of Shillong’s most famous and authentic local meals.

2. **Dohneiiong**  
   A Khasi pork curry cooked with black sesame seeds, giving the dish its distinctive rich flavor and dark texture. It is highly popular among local communities.

3. **Tungrymbai**  
   A fermented soybean dish prepared with local spices, onions, and pork. It represents traditional Khasi tribal cuisine and unique northeastern flavors.

4. **Momos and Thukpa**  
   Due to northeastern and Tibetan culinary influences, Shillong is famous for momos, noodle soups, and warm street-food dishes ideal for cold weather.

5. **Local Café Desserts and Coffee**  
   Shillong’s café culture is highly popular among young travelers and includes pastries, coffee, pancakes, and baked snacks served in cozy hill cafés.

## Things to Carry While Travelling and Advisories

### Travel Advisories (min 3, max 6)
- Shillong experiences sudden weather changes and frequent rainfall even outside monsoon season, so carrying rain protection and layered clothing is highly recommended.

- Many tourist attractions around Shillong involve hilly roads, trekking paths, and outdoor exploration, therefore comfortable walking shoes are essential.

- Road travel in Meghalaya can sometimes be slow because of mountain routes and weather conditions, so travelers should keep flexible schedules for day trips.

- Mobile network connectivity can become weak in remote hill areas outside Shillong city, especially during long road journeys and trekking routes.

### Packing Checklist

#### Compulsory Items (min 3, max 5)
- Government ID / Passport
- Comfortable trekking or walking shoes
- Mobile phone and charger
- Warm jacket or layered clothing
- Rain protection (umbrella or raincoat)

#### Optional Items (max 8, least 4)
- Power bank
- Camera
- Sunglasses
- Reusable water bottle
- Basic medicines
- Small backpack
- Trekking gloves
- Flashlight
