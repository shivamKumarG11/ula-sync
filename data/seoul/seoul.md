# Seoul

## Description
Seoul is the capital city of South Korea and one of Asia’s most technologically advanced and culturally vibrant metropolitan cities. The city combines futuristic skyscrapers, high-speed technology, luxury shopping districts, and modern entertainment with centuries-old palaces, temples, and traditional Korean culture. Seoul is globally famous for K-pop, Korean dramas, street food markets, fashion, skincare products, and nightlife. The city offers an exciting mix of history, innovation, café culture, mountain landscapes, and urban experiences, making it one of the most visited destinations in East Asia. Travelers visit Seoul for its efficient transportation system, safety, shopping, food experiences, cultural attractions, and unique combination of tradition and modernity.

## Best Time to Visit

- Recommended Months: March to May and September to November
- Why This Time is Best: These months provide comfortable temperatures, clear skies, cherry blossom or autumn foliage views, and pleasant conditions for sightseeing, shopping, walking tours, and outdoor cultural activities. Summers can be humid while winters become extremely cold.

### Budget Breakdown (Realistic Expectations and observations)
A realistic traveler in Seoul can expect daily expenses around ₩420,000–₩450,000 ($300–$320), depending on accommodation type and shopping preferences. Accommodation forms one of the largest expenses, especially in tourist-heavy districts such as Myeongdong, Hongdae, and Gangnam. Food costs remain moderate because Seoul offers everything from affordable street food and convenience-store meals to premium Korean barbecue restaurants. Public transportation is highly efficient, affordable, and one of the best ways to reduce travel costs. Shopping, skincare purchases, and entertainment experiences such as K-pop events or themed cafés can significantly increase daily spending. Travelers can save money by purchasing transportation cards and staying slightly outside central shopping districts.

## Popular Food Items to Eat (max 5, least 3)
1. **Kimchi**  
   Korea’s most famous traditional dish made from fermented vegetables, usually cabbage and radish, mixed with chili, garlic, and seasonings. It is served with almost every Korean meal.

2. **Korean BBQ (Samgyeopsal)**  
   One of Seoul’s most popular dining experiences where diners grill pork or beef at the table and eat it with sauces, lettuce wraps, and side dishes.

3. **Tteokbokki**  
   A spicy street-food dish prepared using chewy rice cakes cooked in sweet and spicy red chili sauce. It is especially popular in night markets.

4. **Bibimbap**  
   A mixed rice dish topped with vegetables, meat, egg, chili paste, and sesame oil. It is one of Korea’s most internationally recognized meals.

5. **Korean Fried Chicken**  
   Famous for its crispy texture and flavorful sauces, Korean fried chicken is widely enjoyed alongside beer in Seoul’s nightlife districts.

## Things to Carry While Travelling and Advisories

### Travel Advisories (min 3, max 6)
- Seoul experiences cold winters and hot humid summers, therefore travelers should carefully check seasonal weather conditions before packing.

- Public transportation is highly efficient and cashless systems are widely used, so carrying a T-money transportation card is highly recommended.

- Many restaurants and local markets primarily use Korean language menus, therefore translation apps can be useful for smoother food and shopping experiences.

- Popular tourist attractions and shopping districts become crowded during weekends and evenings, especially in Myeongdong, Hongdae, and Gangnam areas.

### Packing Checklist

#### Compulsory Items (min 3, max 5)
- Passport and travel documents
- Comfortable walking shoes
- Mobile phone and charger
- International payment cards
- Weather-appropriate clothing

#### Optional Items (max 8, least 4)
- Power bank
- Universal travel adapter
- Portable Wi-Fi or SIM card
- Camera
- Sunglasses
- Reusable water bottle
- Basic medicines
- Translation app or phrase guide
